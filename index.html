Carpinchos Are the World’s Largest Rodents

Standing close to 2 feet tall at the shoulder and weighing up to 150 pounds, carpinchos (Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris) are the largest rodents in the world.

They Are Semiaquatic

Carpinchos have partially webbed feet, making them great swimmers. They are able to completely submerge themselves for up to five minutes, allowing them to hide from predators like jaguars, caimans, and anacondas.

They Live in Groups

Carpinchos are highly social animals that live in groups of around 10 to as many as 30 individuals. The groups are stable and work together to defend their habitat.

They Have Unique Vocalizations

Carpinchos are highly communicative with members of their groups. They make unique sounds to share important information — warning of danger, signaling a move, and keeping track of their young.

They Eat Plants

Carpinchos are a vegetarian mammal species. These herbivores feed primarily on aquatic plants, grasses, fruits, and bark. Their diet varies with the seasons — but they eat a lot — with adults consuming the equivalent of six to eight pounds per day.

They Also Eat Poop

In order to get the most nutrition out of every meal, carpinchos are autocoprophagous, meaning they eat their own feces. This practice, which they participate in each morning, provides them with bacterial flora essential to proper digestion.
